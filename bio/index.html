Get to Know Me

Short Biography

I was born in Quito, Ecuador and from early on in my life, I had a passion for nature. From collecting pets of every kind to sharing my appreciation for a gigantic beetle with an unwilling cousin, it has been impossible to keep me from the great outdoors.

After spending many field trips and summers traipsing through the jungles and mountains of Ecuador as well as the breathtaking Gal√°pagos Islands, I graduated from San Francisco de Quito University with a Bachelor's degree in Biology in 2014. After spending another year there working in a research lab, I came to United States to puruse a Master's degree in Environmnetal Science and Management at the Bren Schoool at UC Santa Barbara.

While at UC Santa Barbara, I acclimated to the new, yet still appealing scenery of coastal California. While completing challenging and engaging courswork, my amazing team members and I completed our thesis project of developing a web app to assist communities in Mexico in determining the efficacy of their marine reserves, along with a guide book to aid them in using it.

After graduating from UCSB in 2017, I moved to the Phoenix area to start my professional life in the Sonoran Desert. Since November 2017, I have worked with the Central Arizona Conservation Alliance and the Desert Botanical Garden to develop strategies to get the local population more aware of invasive species present in their communities.

Personal Interests

When I'm not working at the Desert Botanical Garden, I enjoy walking along the Green Belt in Scottsdale where I can hear the strange pig-like noises of the Neotropic Cormorants that haunt the local trees.

International travel is also an occasional treat, and I have enjoyed living in Ecuador, France, and the United States (so far!). I enjoy salsa dancing and keeping up with Latin music. I adore chocolate of any kind and in any form, and I couldn't live without coffee. When I get some time, I love to read a good novel, too.

I'm an aspiring painter and gardener, and one day I'd like to start writing children's literature about nature.
